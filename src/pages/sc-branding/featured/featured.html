The President & the Consitution

Perhaps you haven’t noticed, but its election season! The candidates for the United States Presidency, like many other people, seem to interpret the U.S. Constitution as seen through the lens of their beliefs. This month’s Featured Resources recognizes the upcoming Presidential election and the national observance of Constitution Day. We the People (at the Auraria Library) have selected materials to share that allow research into the many facets of the document that guides countless national legal issues, and its multi-faceted relationship to the highest office in the U.S.

And hey! Come join in a free Constitution Day round table on Friday, September 16th, 10 AM, Room 2600 in the CU Student Commons building.The program, “Assuming the Presidency: From the Constitution to 2016,” will feature a panel of experts discussing the relationship between the Constitution and the presidency, with a particular eye toward the upcoming U.S. Presidential election. Enjoy refreshments while you’re there! Open to all Auraria campus students, faculty, and staff.

Featured Book: The Constitution and the American Presidency

by Martin L. Fausold

In this unusual and provocative volume, historians examine the presidencies of Jefferson, Jackson, Lincoln, Theodore Roosevelt, F. D. R., and Truman, while political scientists assess the contemporary presidency and suggest a range of reforms including fundamental alterations to the balance of power between the presidency and the Congress.

    by David Schultz
